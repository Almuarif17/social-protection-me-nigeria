# About Documentations - HGSF Reading and Summary

## Overview
This folder contains key reference documents and executive materials for the Home Grown School Feeding Programme (HGSF) evaluation and brief. These are comprehensive reports and summaries designed for stakeholders, policymakers, and researchers seeking to understand the HGSF programme's performance and findings.

## Contents

### Evaluated_Home_Grown_School_Feeding_Programme (1).pdf
**Title:** Evaluated Home Grown School Feeding Programme  
**Description:** Comprehensive evaluation report of the HGSF programme  
**Length:** Full-length research document (1.3 MB)  
**Purpose:** Provides detailed analysis, methodology, findings, and recommendations from the evaluation  
**Audience:** Researchers, policymakers, programme administrators, M&E professionals  
**Use Case:**
- Reference document for understanding programme performance
- Source for policy recommendations
- Basis for stakeholder discussions and decisions
- Academic and professional reference material

### NHGSFP_Executive_Summary_Final.pdf
**Title:** NHGSFP (National Home Grown School Feeding Programme) Executive Summary  
**Description:** High-level summary of the evaluation findings  
**Length:** Summary document (921 KB)  
**Purpose:** Provides a condensed version of key findings, without extensive methodological detail  
**Audience:** Busy stakeholders, government officials, quick-reference users  
**Use Case:**
- Quick briefing for decision-makers
- Starting point before diving into full evaluation report
- Foundation for media communications
- Integration into policy briefs

## How to Use These Documents

### For Decision-Making
1. Start with the **Executive Summary** to understand key findings and recommendations
2. Refer to specific sections in the **Full Evaluation** for detailed evidence

### For Research & Analysis
1. Review methodology sections in the full evaluation
2. Use findings to inform secondary analysis
3. Cite appropriately in academic or professional work

### For Stakeholder Communication
1. Extract key statistics from the executive summary
2. Use findings to support programme messaging
3. Reference recommendations for advocacy or policy work

### For Programme Improvement
1. Review recommendations in both documents
2. Identify implementation challenges and successes
3. Plan follow-up M&E activities
